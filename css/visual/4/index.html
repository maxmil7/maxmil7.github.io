4. Positioning

By default elements are layed out top to bottom, left to right Block elements are on a new line and have top bottom margins.

Inline elements only have left and right margins. They do not change the vertical layout of the page.

4.1. Static

Static elements (default positioning) do not have top, bottom, left or right offsets.

They are just layed out according to the default flow.

top, left offset, no effect

4.2. Relative

Relative elements are similar to static elements. Except they can have a top and left offset or a bottom and right offset.

The top or bottom offset is from the element above.

They can overlap other elements to meet offset requirements

top 10px, left 20px

4.3. Absolute

Absolute elements are taken out of the flow, thus, other elements are positioned as if they did not exist.

They are positioned relative to their nearest positioned ancestor (relative, absolute, sticky or fixed but not static)

If a positioned ancestor doesn't exist, it is positioned relative to the root document.

top: 10px, left: 30px

4.4. Fixed

Fixed elements are similar to absolute elements except they are positioned relative to the viewport, even if their parent container is positioned.

Hence, they are not affected by scroll bar position.

fixed element

4.5. Sticky

Sticky elements are similar to relative elements. Except they become 'fixed' once they are scrolled out of the viewport.

They become relative again once inside the viewport

top 10px, left 20px
